## Overview

The gap between how a professional presenter looks on video and how an average webcam renders someone is largely a matter of image processing rather than camera hardware. <a href="#">iGlasses</a> brings the image adjustment layer that professional broadcast cameras apply by default to any webcam on a Mac, improving the output quality through real-time correction of the parameters that most affect perceived <a href="#">video quality</a> in typical office and home lighting conditions.

System-wide operation is iGlasses' most practically important quality. The enhancement is applied to the camera output at the driver level, meaning every application that accesses the camera receives the improved image without any configuration in the individual application. A user who enhances their camera with iGlasses looks better in <a href="#">Zoom</a>, Teams, FaceTime, Google Meet, Skype, and OBS simultaneously — changing the settings in iGlasses updates all of these applications instantly, without opening any of them or navigating their individual camera settings.

The <a href="#">colour temperature</a> control addresses one of the most visible problems with webcam images in typical environments: the colour cast from artificial light sources. Matching the white balance adjustment to the dominant light source — cool for daylight and fluorescent, warm for incandescent and halogen — produces a neutral white in the image that makes skin tones appear natural rather than tinted. Sharpness adjustment compensates for the optical softness of budget webcam lenses, adding <a href="#">edge definition</a> that improves perceived resolution without introducing digital artefacts visible at normal viewing sizes. The combination of brightness, contrast, colour, and sharpness adjustments gives iGlasses the tools to compensate for the most common webcam image deficiencies without requiring hardware upgrades — a meaningfully improved video presence from existing equipment through careful <a href="#">real-time image processing</a> that operates transparently behind every video application the Mac runs.

## Additional Information

Sharpness adjustment is particularly valuable for older or lower-cost webcams whose lenses produce a soft image that reads as poor quality on the receiving end of a video call. Adding controlled edge sharpening through iGlasses improves perceived resolution noticeably without introducing the halos and artefacts that excessive sharpening creates at the pixel level.

The live preview capability before adjustments go live is an important practical feature for anyone who takes video presence seriously. The ability to evaluate and confirm settings before appearing in a meeting or going live in a stream prevents the need to make adjustments while already visible to an audience.

iGlasses' compatibility with the full range of Mac camera hardware — from built-in FaceTime cameras to professional USB webcams and HDMI capture cards — means its benefits are not limited to any particular hardware configuration. Any camera that macOS can access can be enhanced, making it useful regardless of whether the user has upgraded to external camera hardware or is still using the Mac's built-in camera.
